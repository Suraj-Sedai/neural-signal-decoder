Project Overview

Mission

Decode multichannel EEG-like signals into discrete control classes using engineered neural architectures. This project emphasizes rigorous evaluation of modeling paradigms under controlled conditions.

By systematically evaluating different architectural approaches, we identify the inductive biases that matter most for neural signal decoding, providing insights applicable to real BCI systems.

Model Architectures

LSTM Decoder

Sequential LSTM architecture for time-domain signal processing. Captures temporal dependencies but struggles with frequency-aligned features due to inductive bias mismatch.

Transformer Decoder

Attention-based sequence model with better generalization. Time-domain Transformer shows improvement over LSTM but benefits significantly from frequency-domain features.

Spectral MLP

Bandpower-based MLP operating on frequency-aligned features. Achieves highest accuracy by leveraging established neuroscience priors about EEG representation.

Key Results & Findings

Bandpower-Based Transformer

Achieves 85% test accuracy by combining Transformer architecture with frequency-aligned bandpower features. This represents the best architectural-feature alignment.

Inductive Bias Mismatch

Time-domain LSTM and Transformer show systematic performance degradation due to inductive bias mismatch with the frequency-dominant class separability in EEG signals.

Feature Engineering Matters

Frequency-aligned features (bandpower) dramatically outperform raw time-domain features, validating neuroscience priors and emphasizing domain knowledge in neural signal processing.

Reproducibility

Complete modular pipeline with seed control and documentation enables full reproducibility. All experimental conditions and hyperparameters are clearly specified.
